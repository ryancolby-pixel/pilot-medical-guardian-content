Protect your FAA medical certificate.

A privacy-first iOS app that keeps your medical records, renewal timeline, Special Issuance paperwork, and MedXPress prep organized — so you walk into every exam ready. Your health data stays on your device and your own iCloud. The developer can never see it.

Built so we can't see your data

Your records sync only through your own iCloud private database. There's no Pilot Medical Guardian server holding your health, disclosure, or Special Issuance data — that isn't a promise to behave, it's how the app is built.

What it does

Tracks your certificate's expiration from the real 14 CFR rules, charts your health metrics against the FAA's own cited thresholds, organizes your doctor visits and medications to match the MedXPress questions, autofills the parts of MedXPress you'd otherwise retype, surfaces the FAA's CACI worksheets when a new diagnosis might qualify, tracks your Special Issuance requirements, and helps you find an AME.

Why I built this

I'm a pilot at a major U.S. airline. Over the years I watched friends — healthy, capable aviators — get blindsided not by their health, but by the paperwork around it: a renewal that slipped, a Special Issuance requirement that got missed, a MedXPress answer that didn't match last cycle's. Your medical certificate is one of the few things that can ground a career overnight, and almost no one has a system for protecting it.

So I built the one I wanted — private by design, grounded in the FAA's own published rules, and built so that I (or anyone else) can never see your data. It keeps you organized; your AME still makes every certification decision.

— Ryan, founder of Pilot Medical Guardian
